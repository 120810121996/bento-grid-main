E-commerce customer behavior analysis

This project analyzes the behavior of an e-commerce platform's customers, based on a dataset comprising 350 customers and 11 variables (demographics, spending, satisfaction, etc.). The aim is to identify insights for improving customer experience and marketing strategies. Exploratory analysis reveals that Gold customers (spending an average of $1250) and San Francisco customers ( $1459) and San Francisco customers ($1459) are the biggest buyers. Men spend slightly more than women, and satisfied customers buy more items. Linear regression shows that age, city and number of items purchased influence spending. Human Action Recognition

The aim of this project is to develop a model for recognizing human actions in videos (waving, writing, sitting, walking...) by comparing two deep learning architectures architectures: a 3D CNN model built from scratch and a model based on MobileNetV2 with LSTM layers (transfer learning). The results show that the transfer learning approach clearly outperforms the 3D CNN model, with an accuracy of 95% vs. 72% in test in test. The more efficient and generalizable MobileNet+LSTM the best solution for this task, paving the way for future improvements such as the integration of attention attention mechanisms or optimization for real-time use.

Tomato Leaf Disease Detection

The goal of this project is to develop an automated system for detecting and classifying tomato leaf diseases from images. We trained the YOLOv8 model to accurately identify 10 distinct disease categories: Tomato mosaic virus, Target Spot, Bacterial spot, Tomato Yellow Leaf Curl Virus, Late blight, Leaf Mold, Early blight, Spider mites (Two-spotted spider mite), Septoria leaf spot, as well as healthy leaves (Tomato healthy). Leveraging advanced computer vision and deep learning techniques, the model achieved an impressive 92% accuracy in distinguishing specific disease symptoms (spots, discoloration, mold patterns, etc.). This high-performance system enables farmers to rapidly diagnose infections, implement targeted treatments, and minimize crop yield losses, ultimately supporting more sustainable and productive agricultural practices. The model's strong performance demonstrates the effectiveness of YOLOv8 architecture for plant disease recognition tasks.
